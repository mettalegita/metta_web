### Building a Strong Foundation – Piano Technique Essentials

Whether you're just starting your piano journey or looking to refine your advanced skills, developing proper technique is fundamental to musical expression and injury prevention. As a pianist who teaches students of all levels, I believe that technique should serve musicality, not the other way around.

### The Fundamentals of Hand Positioning

Proper hand positioning forms the basis of all piano playing. The goal is to create a natural, relaxed posture that allows for maximum control and expressiveness:

**Hand Shape:** Maintain a curved, natural arch with relaxed fingers
**Wrist Position:** Keep wrists level and flexible, avoiding tension
**Arm Weight:** Use natural arm weight rather than pressing down
**Thumb Placement:** Position thumbs comfortably under the palm

### Progressive Skill Development

Technique building should be systematic and progressive, moving from basic coordination to advanced expression:

**Finger Independence:** Exercises to develop individual finger strength and control
**Scales and Arpeggios:** Building fluency in all keys through systematic practice
**Chord Progressions:** Developing harmonic awareness and voice leading
**Articulation Control:** Mastering staccato, legato, and dynamic variations
